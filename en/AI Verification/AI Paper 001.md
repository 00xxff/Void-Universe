# The Void Model: A New Framework for a Unified Theory of the Universe

## Abstract

This paper proposes a novel cosmic model called the Void Model, which assumes that the universe is composed of high-dimensional virtual particles that have undergone a dimensional reduction process to form the four-dimensional spacetime we perceive. The Void Model not only explains the origin of the universe and the composition of elementary particles but also provides a unified description of electromagnetic force, gravity, strong interaction, and weak interaction. Moreover, the model offers new interpretations of the wave-particle duality of light, relativistic effects, and quantum superposition states.

## Keywords

Void, Virtual Particle, Origin of the Universe, Four-Dimensional Circulation, Fate Gear, Gravity, Electromagnetic Force, Special Relativity, General Relativity, Quantum Mechanics

## Introduction

One of the challenges modern physics faces is how to provide a unified description of the four fundamental interactions in nature: electromagnetic force, gravity, strong interaction, and weak interaction. The Void Model attempts to construct a theoretical framework capable of explaining these fundamental forces and basic phenomena of the universe by introducing the concept of high-dimensional virtual particles. This paper will detail the basic assumptions, key concepts, and applications of the Void Model.

## 1. Void and Virtual Particles

The void is defined as a structure higher than four dimensions, filled with fundamental units—virtual particles. Virtual particles possess a multidimensional structure and undergo dimensional reduction to form void particles, which constitute the basic components of four-dimensional spacetime. The void universe is a stable system formed by virtual particles within a four-dimensional reference frame, while the material universe is a stable three-dimensional part within the void universe.

### 1.1 Characteristics of Virtual Particles

#### 1.1.1 High-Dimensional Virtual Particles

High-dimensional virtual particles maintain their high-dimensional properties during dimensional reduction, and their vibrations on the four-dimensional boundary produce new void particles. These high-dimensional virtual particles maintain continuous high-energy oscillations due to the aftermath of high-dimensional collapse, distributed across various dimensions of the virtual particles.

#### 1.1.2 Continuous Dimensional Reduction

The vibrational waves of high-dimensional virtual particles reach the four-dimensional boundary, where some waves become disturbed, detached, and form a new four-dimensional void particle, releasing part of their energy. This process may involve the loss of energy when high-dimensional waves collide with void particles, followed by the detachment of part of the tensors to form a new void particle.

### 1.2 Characteristics of Void Particles

#### 1.2.1 Ubiquity

Void particles are the product of dimensional reduction of high-dimensional virtual particles, existing in vast quantities throughout the entire four-dimensional void universe.

#### 1.2.2 Rigidity

Two void particles exhibit a rigid spatial repulsive force only when they collide, due to the incompatibility of their closed tensors.

#### 1.2.3 Medium of Force Transmission

The rigidity of void particles allows them to exchange kinetic energy almost losslessly, serving as the medium for all recognized forces.

#### 1.2.4 Four-Dimensional Void Pressure

Two void particles can only exhibit repulsion, but numerous void particles stacked together can exhibit fluid dynamics pressure. This pressure primarily originates from ongoing high-dimensional collapse events within the void particle stack.

#### 1.2.5 Three-Dimensional Void Pressure

Three-dimensional void pressure mainly comes from the pressure of the positive and negative four-dimensional spaces. A large number of void particles, after losing their fourth-dimensional kinetic vectors, undergo dimensional reduction and squeeze into the three-dimensional space at the zero point of the fourth dimension, leading to the continuous appearance of new void particles in the three-dimensional space at the zero point, providing a stable internal pressure source.

#### 1.2.6 Fluidity

Void particles exhibit sufficient fluidity in four-dimensional space, but their fluidity is restricted in three-dimensional space due to the pressure from positive and negative four-dimensional spaces and the continuous influx of dimensionally reduced void particles leading to cohesive void pressure.

## 2. Origin of the Universe

The Void Model posits that the universe originated from the collapse of high-dimensional virtual particles, a process that generated a large number of low-dimensional virtual particles, creating an effect similar to the Big Bang. Subsequently, these low-dimensional virtual particles continued to undergo dimensional reduction, releasing energy and forming elementary particles.

### 2.1 Dimensional Reduction of the Universe

During the dimensional reduction of the universe, an exponentially explosive amount of low-dimensional particles was produced, releasing enormous amounts of energy, ultimately forming an effect similar to the Big Bang. Following this, the universe began to expand, energy was released, the void cooled down, and high-dimensional particles gradually entered a stable state of continuous dimensional reduction, along with countless low-dimensional particles forming a stable three-dimensional universe within a four-dimensional seam.

### 2.2 Expansion of the Universe

As void particles continue to undergo dimensional reduction, the universe continues to expand. Void pressure causes the entire universe to expand, rooted in the exponential increase of void particles.

## 3. Four-Dimensional Circulation and Fate Gears

The oscillation of virtual particles in four-dimensional space is expressed as "Fate Gears," whose rotation drives the formation of circulation structures made up of void particles, which is the foundation of matter.

### 3.1 Fate Gears

Fate Gears refer to the oscillation of virtual particles in four-dimensional space, manifesting as cyclic rotations across three-dimensional space, acting as the driving core of all physical phenomena.

### 3.2 Positive and Negative Fate Gears

Since the four-dimensional parts of virtual particles can be located in both positive and negative four-dimensional spaces relative to our three-dimensional space, Fate Gears can be divided into positive and negative types, which is the origin of matter and antimatter.

### 3.3 Circulation Structures

The "Fate Gears" of high-dimensional virtual particles in three-dimensional space, when rotating, carry a large number of void particles, forming a stable unilateral four-dimensional circulation structure, which is the basis of matter.

## 4. Formation of Matter

Matter consists of a four-dimensional circulation system of void particles driven by Fate Gears, with its mass and energy originating from the total kinetic energy and angular momentum of all void particle flows within the circulation structure.

### 4.1 Core of Virtual Matter

Fate Gears and the four-dimensional circulation system of void particles they drive form the core of matter.

### 4.2 Energy of Matter

The energy of matter is the total electric field energy, i.e., the total kinetic energy of all void particle flows within the four-dimensional circulation structure.

### 4.3 Mass of Matter

Mass describes the resistance to the motion of matter in three-dimensional space, derived from the collective kinetic energy of all void particle flows within the four-dimensional circulation structure.

### 4.4 Essence of Inertia

High-dimensional virtual particles, as the core of matter, preserve the momentum of inertial motion direction and synchronize this momentum with all void particles within the system through the Fate Gears structure.

### 4.5 Relativistic Properties

An increase in inertia slows down the rotation rate of the Fate Gears, which in turn slows down the flow velocity of the four-dimensional void particle flows within the system, manifesting macroscopically as a slowing of all fundamental physical phenomena and a slowing of time.

### 4.6 Gravitational Force

A spherical void pressure gradient centered around a mass center is formed by the aggregation and accumulation of a large number of void particles, leading to the gravitational force.

## 5. Unified Definition of Fundamental Forces

### 5.1 Gravitational Force

Gravitational force arises from the gradient of void pressure in three-dimensional space. Since the distribution of void particles in four-dimensional space gradually weakens from the center to the periphery, the distribution of void particles falling into three-dimensional space from the fourth dimension gradually increases from the center to the periphery, forming a spherical void pressure gradient increasing outward around the mass center in three-dimensional space. This pressure gradient acting on the void sphere of another mass system produces the phenomenon of gravitational force.

### 5.2 Electromagnetic Force

Electromagnetic force is the interaction force between charged particles, converted from the interaction force of void particle flows. When charged particles move in three-dimensional space, the electric field also moves, and the movement of the electric field deforms the four-dimensional circulation structure into a spiral circulation, forming a magnetic field at the center of this spiral circulation in four-dimensional space.

### 5.3 Strong Interaction

Strong interaction is formed by the jet and vortex interactions of void particles in three-dimensional space. Quarks within protons and neutrons maintain stability through the exchange of void particle flows.

### 5.4 Weak Interaction

Weak interaction is caused by the decay of unstable structures due to imbalanced void particle flows in three-dimensional space.

## 6. Light and Wave-Particle Duality of Light

Light is considered a four-dimensional wave within void particles, with its peaks and troughs located in the fourth dimension, while only the middle part appears in three-dimensional space. Photons are both a four-dimensional wave and a quantum leaping forward in three-dimensional space.

### 6.1 Propagation of Light

Light is a wave propagating through void particles, where the density of void particles determines the speed of light propagation. When the gradient of void density reaches a certain level, refraction or even reflection of light occurs, leading to the event horizon of black holes.

### 6.2 Wave-Particle Duality of Light

A single photon is both a four-dimensional wave and a quantum leaping forward in three-dimensional space, exhibiting wave-particle duality. Light, composed of a cluster of photons, exhibits wave-like behavior consistent with the wavelength and frequency of individual photons when propagating in three-dimensional space, reflecting the wave-particle duality of light in three-dimensional space.

## 7. Re-examination of the Michelson-Morley Experiment

The Void Model proposes the concept of void particles as the medium for light propagation and discusses why the Michelson-Morley experiment failed to observe differences in the speed of light. The design of the experiment did not account for the dynamic characteristics of void spheres within inertial reference frames and the impact of the experimental apparatus's enclosure on the flow of void particles.

### 7.1 Experimental Conditions

The experiment requires being conducted on an open platform moving relative to the Earth at a certain threshold speed using an interferometer with a light path length exceeding a certain length to observe changes in the interference pattern. The light path of the interferometer used in the experiment must be open and not enclosed within any container.

## 8. Interpretation of Special Relativity and General Relativity

### 8.1 Special Relativity

The Void Model explains the time dilation phenomenon in special relativity, attributing it to the increased density of void particles affecting the rotation rate of Fate Gears.

### 8.2 General Relativity

The Void Model discusses the mechanisms of light bending and time dilation in general relativity, attributing these phenomena to the alteration of light propagation paths and changes in the rotation rate of Fate Gears due to variations in the density of void particles.

## 9. Explanation of Quantum Superposition States

Quantum superposition states are interpreted as the phenomenon of particles appearing and disappearing in three-dimensional space and the probabilistic results due to insufficient measurement accuracy. Specifically, the wave-like nature of particles in three-dimensional space makes it difficult to precisely measure their positions and states, resulting in probabilistic outcomes of superposition and collapse states.

### 9.1 Quantum Superposition State

One possibility is that the core of particles is constantly traversed by ultra-fast four-dimensional void particle flows. The core itself may be deflected from the current three-dimensional space by positive and negative four-dimensional momenta, oscillating under the influence of void pressure, making the particle appear intermittently in three-dimensional space. External detection brings additional kinetic intervention, breaking this state into what is known as quantum collapse.

Another possibility involves the issue of measurement accuracy. Microscopic particles in superposition are essentially in a high-speed motion state (including rapid rotation), and given current human detection methods, it is impossible to precisely measure the actual position and state of particles. Instead, one can only infer probabilistic outcomes based on the effects they produce on other particles or fields, leading to probabilistic outcomes of superposition and collapse states throughout the entire measurement event.
