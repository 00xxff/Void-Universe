## Abstract

This paper proposes a non-compactified high-dimensional cosmological model that divides reality into two levels: the supermembrane universe and the particle universe. The supermembrane universe is the origin layer, consisting of a primordial supermembrane of infinite-dimensional hypersphere $S^\infty$ — the only stable geometric configuration derived from the principle of least action that minimizes membrane tension. A symmetry-breaking event generates fluctuations on the hypersphere, causing the supermembrane to deform to accommodate these fluctuations, forming paired convex and concave structures with opposite phases. The total energy is $(+E_0, -E_0)$ and strictly cancels to zero. The split submembranes are homeomorphic to the primordial supermembrane, both being infinite-dimensional hyperspheres $S^\infty$, differing only in their residual fluctuation states. The particle universe is the emergent layer, defined on a four-dimensional slice of the supermembrane universe. It is formed by the aggregation of numerous low-energy submembranes and some active submembranes, creating a relatively ordered material universe. Void particles are low-energy submembranes with nearly quiescent fluctuations — their cross-sections at the particle universe level are three-dimensional spheres $S^3$, but may contain finer fractal fluctuations internally, belonging to the story of deeper cosmic levels. The energy of the particle universe originates from fluctuations on high-dimensional submembranes with dimensions greater than 3. Through collisions with void particles, fluctuation energy from the supermembrane universe is injected into the particle universe, manifesting as tangential motion (three-dimensional movement, corresponding to kinetic energy) and radial motion (four-dimensional oscillation, corresponding to thermal energy).

**Keywords**: Supermembrane Universe; Particle Universe; Void Particle; Ontological Framework; Dimensional Emergence

## 1 Introduction

One of the core challenges facing modern theoretical physics is reconciling the continuous spacetime geometry of general relativity with the discrete features of quantum mechanics. String theory attempts to unify them through compactified extra dimensions [1], while loop quantum gravity directly assumes spatial discreteness [2]. However, neither clearly distinguishes between "the universe itself" and "the universe we observe."

This paper proposes a crucial distinction: the supermembrane universe and the particle universe represent different levels of reality. The supermembrane universe is the origin layer of infinite-dimensional hypersphere $S^\infty$, containing the primordial supermembrane and all its submembranes. The particle universe is an emergent layer on a four-dimensional slice of the supermembrane universe, formed by the aggregation of numerous low-energy and active submembranes — it is the observable material world. This distinction clarifies the relativity of concepts such as dimension, energy, and entropy, and identifies two modes of particle motion: tangential motion (kinetic energy) and radial motion (thermal energy). This paper focuses solely on the ontological architecture and the geometric-energy logic of dimensional emergence, without addressing dynamical reduction of low-energy effective fields.

## 2 Supermembrane Universe: The Origin Layer

### 2.1 Geometric Structure of the Primordial Supermembrane

**Definition 1** The primordial supermembrane $\Sigma$ is an infinite-dimensional hypersphere (Hilbert Sphere, $S^\infty$), satisfying:

- **Dimension**: $\dim \Sigma = \infty$ (countable infinite cardinality $\aleph_0$)
- **Geometry**: $\Sigma$ is a unit sphere embedded in infinite-dimensional Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$:

  $$
  S^\infty = \{ x \in \mathcal{H} : |x| = 1 \}
  $$

  with constant positive curvature.
- **Topology**: $\Sigma$ is a complete closed manifold with no boundary.
- **Symmetry**: $\Sigma$ is a homogeneous space with isometry group $O(\infty)$, representing the maximally symmetric space.

> **Note (Physical Idealization and Observables)**: In the standard topology of infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, the unit sphere is bounded and closed but non-compact. In the ontological framework, $S^\infty$ serves as a mathematical idealization of the pre-geometric phase. Physical observables depend only on projections of finite excitation modes onto low-dimensional slices. The infinite-dimensional non-compactness is handled through **effective spectral truncation**: observables are defined as limits of finite mode projections, which converge to stable values under weak topology. Positive curvature holds only for the effective excitation subspace, without requiring a strictly Riemannian structure for the full space.

**Theorem 1** (Minimal Action Configuration)
Based on the principle of least action, to minimize its membrane tension potential energy, the primordial supermembrane necessarily evolves into a geometric configuration with optimal surface area to volume ratio. For closed membranes, the action is proportional to its world volume (Nambu-Goto action):

$$
S_{\text{membrane}} \propto \int d^{N+1}\xi \sqrt{-\det g}
$$

Under fixed internal volume or energy constraints, the closed geometric shape that minimizes the action is a manifold with uniformly equal curvature — i.e., a hypersphere:

$$
\delta S_{\text{membrane}} = 0 \implies \Sigma \cong S^\infty
$$

**Physical Image**: Just as a soap bubble naturally forms a spherical shape without external forces, the primordial supermembrane necessarily tends toward the highest symmetry ground state configuration — the infinite-dimensional hypersphere — to minimize its tension potential energy.

### 2.2 Symmetry Breaking and Paired Fluctuations

**Postulate 1** (Symmetry Breaking Event)
At some primordial moment, a symmetry-breaking event occurs on $\Sigma$, generating a fluctuation field $\Phi: \Sigma \to \mathbb{R}$. To accommodate these fluctuations, the supermembrane deforms, forming paired convex and concave structures with opposite phases:

$$
\Phi_+ = v + \phi_+(x) \quad \text{(convex wave)}
$$

$$
\Phi_- = -v + \phi_-(x) \quad \text{(concave wave)}
$$

where $\phi_+$ and $\phi_-$ satisfy $\phi_+ = -\phi_-$. On the hypersphere $S^\infty$, the fluctuation field can be expanded into hyperspherical harmonics $Y_{l,\mathbf{m}}$. Paired fluctuations correspond to opposite phase excitations of the $l=1$ mode.

**Theorem 2** (Initial Energy Zero-Sum)
Paired fluctuations generated by symmetry breaking carry opposite energies:

$$
E_+ = +E_0, \quad E_- = -E_0
$$

satisfying $E_+ + E_- = 0$.

> **Note**: For low-dimensional observers, the initial energy $E_0$ is formally divergent, corresponding to the sum of all excitable spectral modes of the primordial supermembrane. However, due to the strict paired occurrence, the total energy of the supermembrane universe remains zero.

### 2.3 Split Products are Homeomorphic to the Primordial Supermembrane

**Theorem 3** (Homeomorphism of Split Products)
The sub-supermembrane $\Sigma'$ produced by splitting from the primordial supermembrane is homeomorphic to the primordial supermembrane $\Sigma$:

$$
\Sigma' \cong \Sigma \cong S^\infty, \quad \dim \Sigma' = \infty
$$

**Proof**: The splitting process separates an open set $U$ from $\Sigma$. Due to the topological stability of the infinite-dimensional Hilbert sphere, this open set becomes homeomorphic to $\Sigma$ itself after reclosure. Therefore, the separated submembrane retains the same topological structure and dimension as the primordial supermembrane.

**Corollary 1** All submembranes in the supermembrane universe — regardless of the number of splittings or their fluctuation states — are topologically homeomorphic to the primordial supermembrane, all being infinite-dimensional hyperspheres $S^\infty$.
**Corollary 2** The only difference between submembranes lies in their residual surface fluctuation states: some carry active high-energy fluctuations (virtual particles), while others have nearly quiescent fluctuations (void particles).

### 2.4 Infinite Divisibility of Fluctuations

**Theorem 4** (Infinite Divisibility of Fluctuations)
Fluctuations generated by symmetry breaking, as functions on $\Sigma$, form submanifolds of $\Sigma$ through their graphs. Since $\Sigma$ is infinitely divisible, fluctuations can be infinitely split into finer fluctuation modes. In the hyperspherical harmonic expansion, this means progressively exciting higher-order angular quantum number $l$ modes, forming infinitely fine fluctuation structures.

### 2.5 Collision-Induced Splitting and Energy Increment

**Definition 2** A standing wave packet is a triple $(U, \psi, \mathcal{E})$, where $U \subset \Sigma$ is an open set, $\psi$ is a solution to the wave equation on $U$, and $\mathcal{E} = \int_U T_{00} d\mu$ is the energy of the standing wave packet.

**Postulate 2** When two or more standing wave packets (or supermembranes) collide, if the collision region $U \subset \Sigma$ satisfies $T_{AB}T^{AB}|_U \ge T_c^2$ ($T_c$ is a universal critical constant), then $U$ may detach from the parent body and become an independent sub-supermembrane.

**Theorem 5** (Energy Increment Conservation)
In each collision or splitting event, newly generated fluctuation energy always appears in pairs, such that the total energy increment of the supermembrane universe is strictly zero:

$$
\Delta E_{\text{total}} = \sum (+\Delta E_i) + \sum (-\Delta E_i) = 0
$$

**Proof**: The collision process excites new fluctuation modes, which necessarily appear as opposite phase pairs. This is a direct consequence of the linear superposition principle of wave equations and the quadratic form of the energy-momentum tensor. Local energy conservation requires that newly generated positive and negative energies precisely cancel.

**Corollary 3** The total energy of the supermembrane universe always equals the energy generated by the initial symmetry-breaking event: $E_0 + (-E_0) = 0$. All subsequent splitting and collision events do not alter the total energy.

### 2.6 Relativity of Energy Scale

**Definition 3** (Effective Splitting Chain and Dynamical Freezing)
Let the initial energy of the symmetry-breaking event be $E_0$. Fluctuation modes undergo cascading splitting through collisions, with energy scales decaying by geometric factors. This process is not mathematically infinite, but a **dynamical renormalization flow** constrained by high-dimensional geometric rigidity and mode coupling. When the characteristic scale approaches the minimum effective scale $\ell_{\text{min}}$, the splitting chain freezes at a low-energy fixed point. The residual energy $E_{\text{res}}$ is a finite value under effective truncation.

**Theorem 6** (Relativity of Energy Scale)
For low-dimensional observers, $E_0$ is formally infinite. The observable energy $E_{\text{obs}}$ is the tiny net positive energy remaining on a specific four-dimensional slice after cascading splitting and dynamical freezing:

$$
E_{\text{obs}} \sim E_0 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{four-dimensional slice measure}}{\text{infinite-dimensional space measure}} \right) \cdot \mathcal{F}_{\text{cascade}} \to \text{finite value}
$$

where $\mathcal{F}_{\text{cascade}}$ is the splitting chain freezing factor. On the hypersphere $S^\infty$, we use normalized projection measure — considering only projections of finite excitation modes onto the slice. Therefore, although the total energy of the supermembrane universe is always zero and the increment is zero for each event, finite positive energy can still be observed in the particle universe.

## 3 Void Particles: Low-Energy States with Quiescent Fluctuations

### 3.1 Definition of Void Particles

**Definition 4** A void particle is a submembrane cross-section in the supermembrane universe satisfying the following conditions:

- **Ontology**: Topologically homeomorphic to the primordial supermembrane, being an infinite-dimensional hypersphere $S^\infty$
- **Formation Mechanism**: This $S^\infty$ submembrane immerses into a four-dimensional slice $L_4$ and intersects with the three-dimensional membrane universe $\mathcal{M}_3$
- **Cross-Section Configuration**: Since the parent body is a hypersphere, its cross-section with the four-dimensional slice manifests as a three-dimensional sphere $S^3$ configuration: $\Sigma_{\text{void}} \cap L_4 \cong S^3$
- **Effective Radius**: $R = \ell_{\text{min}}$ (minimum effective scale)
- **Energy State**: Surface fluctuation amplitude $\langle |\nabla \Phi|^2 \rangle < \varepsilon_c$, meaning fluctuations are nearly quiescent

> **Note (Scale Anchoring)**: $\ell_{\text{min}}$ is the ultraviolet cutoff scale of the ontological framework, matching the Planck length $\ell_P$ in low-energy phenomenology. This is not an a priori postulate but a boundary anchoring condition between effective theory and observation. Rigorous derivation requires solving higher-dimensional fluctuation power-geometric dissipation balance equations, belonging to subsequent branch work.

**Theorem 7** (Cross-Section Topology Inheritance)
If the immersed manifold $\Sigma \cong S^\infty$, for stable immersed states with quiescent fluctuations, the stable cross-section $\Sigma \cap L_4$ on the low-dimensional slice $L_4$ maintains spherical topology $S^k$. For four-dimensional slices, $k=3$, i.e., $S^3$. For immersed states carrying active fluctuations, cross-sections can exhibit non-trivial topology (winding, knotting, etc.).

### 3.2 Void Particle Sea

Void particles are randomly densely packed in the infinite-dimensional space $\mathbb{R}^\infty$ of the supermembrane universe, with centroid positions $\{X_i\}$ satisfying $|X_i - X_j| \ge 2R$.

**Definition 5** The void particle sea $Q$ is the closure of the set of void particle centroids: $Q = \overline{\{X_i\}}$.

**Theorem 8** (Packing Density)
In the infinite-dimensional limit, the void particle packing density $\eta_\infty$ equals the maximum packing density of three-dimensional spheres in infinite-dimensional space:

$$
\eta_\infty = \lim_{M\to\infty} \frac{M \cdot V_{S^3}}{\text{Vol}(\text{conv}\{X_1,\dots,X_M\})} = 0.6366\ldots
$$

The void particle sea is the region with the most quiescent fluctuations in the supermembrane universe, forming the base for particle universe generation.

### 3.3 Deeper Fractal Fluctuations

**Note**: Void particles may contain finer fractal fluctuations internally — i.e., excitations of higher-order hyperspherical harmonics. However, these fluctuations occur at smaller scales and higher frequencies, corresponding to deeper cosmic structures. Their interaction with the particle universe level is negligible. Therefore, in our effective theory, void particles are regarded as "quiescent fluctuation" low-energy states. This reflects the hierarchical nested structure of the universe: each cosmic level has its effective degrees of freedom and physical laws, with deeper fluctuations manifesting as stable entities at higher levels.

## 4 Particle Universe: The Emergent Layer

### 4.1 Four-Dimensional Slice and Intersection

**Definition 6** A four-dimensional slice $L_4$ is a subspace spanned by any four linearly independent directions in $\mathbb{R}^\infty$. The particle universe is defined on a specific four-dimensional slice $L_4$. Entities within this slice are not projections, but intersection sets of $S^\infty$ submembranes with $L_4$.

> **Note (Multiple Slices and Our Universe)**: $L_4$ is not unique. The supermembrane universe allows numerous four-dimensional slices, each corresponding to different intersection geometries and fluctuation projection spectra. **Our universe corresponds to only one of these slices that satisfies specific dynamical stability conditions (see Theorem 11).** Other slices may quickly dissipate into disordered void seas due to parameter deviations from the critical window, or undergo topological collapse, unable to sustain long-term ordered particle universe structures.

Denote $Q_4$ as the set of void particle cross-section centroids. Its number density: $\bar{\rho}_4 = \eta(4) \rho_0$, where $\eta(4) = 0.60$ is the maximum packing density factor of $S^3$ in four-dimensional space.

### 4.2 Three-Dimensional Membrane Interface

In $Q_4$, the packing layers of void particle cross-sections ($S^3$) form a common contact surface under four-dimensional pressure gradients.

**Definition 7** The three-dimensional membrane $\mathcal{M}_3$ is the maximum gradient surface of the pressure field $P(x)$ on $Q_4$:

$$
\mathcal{M}_3 = \{x \in Q_4 : |\nabla P(x)| = \max\}
$$

**Physical Image**: The three-dimensional membrane $\mathcal{M}_3$ is the "space" we observe. Void particles ($S^3$) immerse and penetrate this three-dimensional membrane. What we observe on $\mathcal{M}_3$ is the secondary cross-section of $S^3$ with $\mathcal{M}_3$ (i.e., $S^3 \cap \mathcal{M}_3 \cong S^2$, appearing as spherical surfaces or particle tracks).

### 4.3 Composition of the Particle Universe

The particle universe contains two types of entities (both immersed cross-sections of $S^\infty$ on four-dimensional slices):

- **Void Particles (Low-Energy Supermembranes)**: Ontology $S^\infty$ (nearly quiescent fluctuations), cross-section $S^3$, forming the base medium of the particle universe.
- **Virtual Particles (Active Supermembranes)**: Ontology $S^\infty$ (carrying active high-dimensional fluctuations), immersing in $L_4$ with non-trivial topology. Their surface active fluctuations drive surrounding void particle flows to form fluid topologies such as vortices and vortex tubes, and combine with each other to form composite structures. Overall, they exhibit complex topologies of winding, knotting, or high-dimensional penetration systems. These complex topologies manifest as "elementary particles" and their associated quantum numbers on the three-dimensional membrane $\mathcal{M}_3$.

### 4.5 Energy Source of the Particle Universe

**Injection Mechanism**:

1. Virtual particles ($S^\infty$ immersed states carrying active fluctuations) move within the four-dimensional slice $L_4$.
2. Cross-sections of virtual particles intersect and collide with cross-sections of void particles (Intersection Collision).
3. Collisions cause fluctuation energy on the $S^\infty$ surface to transfer through cross-section contact points.
4. This energy transforms into tangential motion (kinetic energy) and radial oscillation (thermal energy, i.e., breathing vibration of cross-sections in the fourth dimension) of cross-sections on the three-dimensional membrane $\mathcal{M}_3$.

### 4.6 Energy Balance of the Particle Universe

Total energy of the particle universe:

$$
E_{\text{particle}} = \int_{\mathcal{M}_3} \left( \frac{1}{2} \rho v_{\parallel}^2 + \frac{1}{2} \rho \omega_{\perp}^2 A^2 + E_{\text{potential}} \right) dV_3
$$

Energy conservation equation:

$$
\frac{dE_{\text{particle}}}{dt} = \mathcal{P} V_3 - \mathcal{L}_{\text{rad}} - \mathcal{L}_{\text{fric}}
$$

**Theorem 10** (Stability of the Particle Universe)
The particle universe can stably exist if and only if the energy injection rate balances the loss rate, and local parameters satisfy critical conditions.

### 4.7 Stability Conditions of the Particle Universe

**Theorem 11** (Stability Conditions and Slice Selection) The particle universe $U \subset \mathcal{M}_3$ can stably exist if and only if local parameters fall within the following critical window:

- Void particle number density $\rho \in [\rho_{c1}, \rho_{c2}]$

- Void particle flow velocity $v < v_{\max}$ (preventing complex structures from being dispersed)

**Critical Window Parameters**:

**Habitable Zone Volume Fraction**:

$$
F_{\text{hab}} = \int_{\rho_{c1}}^{\rho_{c2}} \mathcal{N}(\rho; \bar{\rho}_4, \sigma_\rho) d\rho \approx 2.1 \times 10^{-5}

> **Note**: This window constitutes a **dynamical attractor** in the supermembrane phase space. Only when the background flux of slice orientation, void particle packing density, and active submembrane injection rate simultaneously fall within this window can the three-dimensional membrane interface $\mathcal{M}_3$ maintain long-term pressure balance and topological excitation stability. Our universe is precisely one of the four-dimensional slices selected by this attractor.
