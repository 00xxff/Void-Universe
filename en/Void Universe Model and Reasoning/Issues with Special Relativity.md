### Issues with Special Relativity

Special relativity posits:

1. **Time Dilation**: When one object moves at high speed relative to another, time slows down for the former. That is, less time passes in the moving reference frame compared to the stationary one.
2. **Length Contraction**: When one object moves at high speed relative to another, the length of the former in the direction of motion contracts. This means the size of the object decreases in the direction of movement.
3. **Relativity of Simultaneity**: Two events that occur simultaneously in one inertial reference frame may not occur simultaneously in another frame that is moving relative to the first.
4. **Mass-Energy Equivalence**: Mass and energy can be converted into one another, most famously expressed by Einstein's equation $(E = mc^2)$, where $(E)$ represents energy, $(m)$ is the object's mass, and $(c)$ is the speed of light.

However, I believe that our perception of time relies on certain physical quantities, and when these quantities change under the influence of high-speed motion, it results in a perceived slowing of clocks, giving rise to the illusion of time dilation (this is also the explanation provided by the current model). It is not just the slowing down of some specific quantity used for timing but rather the slowing down of the entire physical system, meaning that the relative motions of all particles slow down literally. The reason is that the kinetic energy in one direction takes away from the available energy in other directions, causing the whole system to behave like a CPU that has been throttled. This is a hardware-level action, and the software running on this hardware does not feel the slowing of time, but it can be observed through comparison with an external reference frame.
