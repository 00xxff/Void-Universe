### Phenomena of General Relativity

#### Deflection of Light

Near a strong gravitational field, light bends. Based on our previous deductions about the generation and propagation of light, light propagates through void particles, and the density of these void particles determines the speed of light. Therefore, we deduce that the spacetime curvature caused by gravity is actually a change in the density of void particles. This density change leads to the bending of the path of light and electromagnetic waves. When the gradient of the void density change reaches a certain level, light will refract or even reflect, which leads to the event horizon of a black hole.

According to this deduction, light is not "sucked" back by a black hole but rather turns back on its own. Perhaps the gravitational pull of a black hole is not enough to prevent matter moving at nearly the speed of light from escaping; it acts more like a prison for light.

The escape velocity calculated by AI using the universal gravitation formula for a black hole is far less than the speed of light. Therefore, general relativity can be said to have been proposed to resolve this contradiction. Isn't this putting the cart before the horse?

#### Time Dilation

In a strong gravitational field, time passes more slowly. Similar to the explanation of phenomena in special relativity, an increase in void pressure leads to an increase in the density of void particles, affecting the rotational speed of the "fate gears," ultimately slowing down the flow speed of void particles in the entire system, creating the illusion of time dilation.

#### Gravitational Waves

In the void ocean filled with void particles, two ultra-large, high-density mass systems, carrying high-density void spheres relatively stationary to themselves, rotate and collide at high speeds. This causes a large number of high-density void particles in three-dimensional space to move at high relative speeds, generating compression and collisions, which eventually propagate outward in the form of longitudinal waves. Since the speed of force transmission between void particles is the speed of light, the propagation speed of gravitational waves is also the speed of light.
