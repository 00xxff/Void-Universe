### Problems of Special Relativity

Special Relativity posits:

1. **Time Dilation**: When an object moves at high speeds relative to another, time for the former slows down. That is, the time experienced in a fast-moving reference frame is less than that experienced in a stationary reference frame.
2. **Length Contraction**: When an object moves at high speeds relative to another, its length in the direction of motion shortens. This means the size of the object in the direction of motion becomes smaller.
3. **Relativity of Simultaneity**: Two events that occur simultaneously in one inertial reference frame may not occur simultaneously in another relatively moving inertial reference frame.
4. **Mass-Energy Equivalence**: Mass and energy can be converted into each other. This concept is famously expressed by Einstein's equation $( E = mc^2 )$, where $( E )$ represents energy, $( m )$ is the object's mass, and $( c )$ is the speed of light.

However, I believe that our understanding of time relies on certain physical quantities, and these quantities change under the influence of certain factors during high-speed motion, leading to a perceived slowing of clock frequency, which gives rise to the illusion of time dilation (this is also the explanation provided by the model). It is not just a slowdown of a specific quantity used for timing but a slowdown of the entire physical system, meaning literally all relative motions of particles slow down. The reason is that the kinetic energy in a single direction takes away the energy available for allocation in other directions, akin to a CPU being downclocked. This is a hardware-level behavior, and the software systems running on this hardware do not feel the slowdown, but it can be observed through comparison with an external reference frame.
