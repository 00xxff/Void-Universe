
### Special Relativity Issues

Special relativity posits:

1. **Time Dilation**: When one object moves at high speed relative to another, time for the moving object slows down. That is, less time passes in a high-speed moving reference frame compared to a stationary one.
2. **Length Contraction**: An object moving at high speed relative to another appears contracted along the direction of motion. This means the size of the object in its direction of travel decreases.
3. **Relativity of Simultaneity**: Two events that occur simultaneously in one inertial reference frame may not be simultaneous in another inertial frame moving relative to the first.
4. **Mass-Energy Equivalence**: Mass and energy are interchangeable, famously expressed by Einstein's mass-energy equation $(E = mc^2)$, where $(E)$ represents energy, $(m)$ the mass of an object, and $(c)$ the speed of light.

I believe that our perception of time relies on certain physical quantities, which change under high-speed motion due to some factors, leading to the physical manifestation of slower time and thus creating the illusion of time dilation (this is also how this model explains it).

In our Void Model, all physical phenomena are based on the Fate Gears at the core of each fundamental particle. Thus, we start from the Fate Gear to deduce backward the causes leading to the effects described by special relativity.

Firstly, let's analyze the cause of relativistic mass increase.

These four-dimensional void circulations constitute complex hyperspace circulation structures within four-dimensional space. Therefore, we can define the resistance coefficient of all void particle circulations in the system against changes in momentum as mass, which describes the cost required to alter the momentum of an object moving in any direction within three-dimensional space.

This mass coefficient is directly proportional to the total momentum of the system's void particle flows.

From experiments related to special relativity, observations showing time dilation effects mostly involve the decay lifetimes of unstable particles, while those for relativistic mass typically come from high-energy particle experiments.

Starting with high-energy particle experiments, assume an electron moving rapidly encounters more void particles head-on. Assuming these void particles are relatively stationary, the relative speed between the Fate Gear and these void particles equals the electron's speed, effectively creating a new void particle circulation around the Fate Gear in three-dimensional space. The faster the electron moves, the faster this void particle flow becomes. When the electron's speed matches its four-dimensional void circulation speed, it creates an equivalent additional void particle circulation system. This implies that changing the electron's state requires twice the energy, meaning its mass has doubled, which should be the cause of relativistic mass increase in special relativity.

Regarding time dilation experiments, most observations concern the decay lifetimes of unstable particles. Faster movement speeds and higher void particle densities mean greater external pressure, allowing the balance to persist longer, corresponding to the time dilation effect in both special and general relativity.
